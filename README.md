<h1>⚖️ Bias and Fairness in Machine Learning</h1>

<h2>🎯 Overview</h2>
This repository contains a high‑distinction (100/100) research essay examining the origins of bias in machine learning systems, the challenges of defining fairness, and the effectiveness of modern mitigation strategies.  
The work combines technical, ethical, and legal perspectives to provide a comprehensive understanding of responsible AI.

<h2>📌 Key Topics Covered</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Historical, societal, and structural sources of ML bias</li>
  <li>Representation, measurement, aggregation, and deployment bias</li>
  <li>Group fairness, individual fairness, and counterfactual fairness</li>
  <li>Pre‑processing, in‑processing, and post‑processing mitigation strategies</li>
  <li>Case studies: COMPAS recidivism tool, Amazon hiring algorithm</li>
  <li>Ethical and legal frameworks (GDPR, AI transparency, accountability)</li>
  <li>Future directions: causal fairness, participatory design, fairness toolkits</li>
</ul>

<h2>📚 Why This Matters</h2>
Machine learning systems increasingly influence high‑stakes decisions such as hiring, healthcare, policing, and lending.  
This essay highlights how biased data and model design can reinforce societal inequalities and how fairness‑aware methods can help prevent harm.
